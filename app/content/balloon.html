Hands-on HAB Satellite Training

Bring a Live HAB Balloon Satellite Launch to Your Campus

QSAT offers a 2–3 day (≈16 hours) experiential program where students design, assemble and launch a student-built satellite payload on a tethered HAB up to 1 km. The program includes ground station setup, live streaming, telemetry & recovery — a complete, safe end-to-end mission experience.

Program
2–3 days • 16 hours • Ideal batch: 40–60 students
Student-assembled satellite kit
Tethered launch (≤1 km) with live video
Deliverables
Balloon + satellite kit, ground station
Full launch video, telemetry & data logs
Certificates & post-event replay

What students gain:

  • Hands-on experience with satellite subsystems, payload testing and telemetry
  • Practical skills in data logging, analysis, and mission operations
  • Team-based project execution to build portfolios for placements and higher studies

Why partner with QSAT? This event enhances experiential learning, helps with NAAC/NBA innovation metrics, and provides excellent campus visibility through media and student showcases.
Logistics & Safety

Venue: indoor hall for training + open ground for tethered launch. QSAT provides trained staff, safety briefings, and recovery. Launch strictly limited to 1 km for safety & regulatory compliance.
